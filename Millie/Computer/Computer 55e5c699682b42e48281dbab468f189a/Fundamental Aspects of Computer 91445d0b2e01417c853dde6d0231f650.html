Fundamental Aspects of Computer

  1. Define a computer. Write down the functions of a computer.

    Computer is an electronic data processing device capable of taking an input data, processing them and displaying the processed result at a very high speed under the control of set of instruction.

    The features of computer are:

    1. Speed
    1. Accuracy
    1. Versatility
    1. Diligence
  1. Define the terms input, processing, and output as used in computer science.

    Input is the raw information entered into a computer from the input device. It is a collection of letter, numbers, images, sounds, videos, etc.

    Process is the operation of data as per the given instruction. It is totally internal process of the computer system

    Output is the processed data given by a computer after data processing. Output is also called result.

  1. What are the limitations of a computer?
    • Computer cannot operate without the instructions given by humans. It is programmed to work effectively, fast and accurately.
    • Computer does not have intelligence of its own to complete the tasks. ...
    • Lack of feeling is another limitation of computer.
    • Computer has no learning power.
  1. What is meant by computer generations? Name the different computer generations.

    The phase wise development of computers on the basis of speed electronic component, performance and specific period is known as computer generations.

    The different computer generations:

    • 1940 – 1956: First Generation – Vacuum Tubes. These early computers used vacuum tubes as circuitry and magnetic drums for memory.
    • 1956 – 1963: Second Generation – Transistors.
    • 1964 – 1971: Third Generation – Integrated Circuits.
    • 1972 – 2010: Fourth Generation – Microprocessors
    • Fifth Generation Computer
  1. List the features of the fourth generation computers.

    The fourth-generation computer are as follows:

    • Use of microprocessor in place of transistors.
    • Processing speed in pico second (Processing of billions of instructions in second)
    • User-friendly computers which used many high level language ( BASIC,C,etc) for programming
    • Use of magnetic storage device as auxiliary storage and semi-conductor as primary memory.
  1. What is an IC?

    An integrated circuit, or IC, is small chip that can function as an amplifier, oscillator, timer, microprocessor, or even computer memory.

  1. Define Artificial Intelligence.

    Artificial Intelligence refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are programmed to think like humans and mimic their actions.

    This device was developed by a Sottish mathematician, John Napier in 1614 AD. He had also published a book 'Rabdologia' which was bout logarithm. It consisted the first table of logarithms which was very helpful in simplifying multiplication of large numbers.

    Napier's Bones considered of a set of bone-rods on which the numbers were engraved. By the combination of these bones, direct multiplication could be done. However, it could be use to divide and extract roots.

  1. Why is Charles Babbage known as the 'Father of Computer'?

    The concept of input, output, processing, and storage used by Charles Babbage in his analytical engine became the basis for the evaluation of using bits in his machine. Since the dream of modern computers has come true only due to Babbage's innovative idea. He is considered as the father of computer science
