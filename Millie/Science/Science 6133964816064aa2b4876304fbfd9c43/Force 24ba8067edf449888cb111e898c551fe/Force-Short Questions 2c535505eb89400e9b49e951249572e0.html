  • Why Newton’s law of gravitation is called universal law?

    Newton's law of gravitation holds true for all objects present in this universe whether the terrestrial or celestial body, the force of gravitation exists everywhere in the universe. Even for small microscopic elements like protons, and electrons in an atom. Newton's law of gravitation holds good. So, it is called universal law.

  • Why is gravitation constant called the universal gravitational constant?

    Gravitational constant is called universal gravitational constant because:

    1. It does not depend upon nature of mass of objects.
    1. It does not depend upon chemical composition of object
    1. It is unaffected by temperature and pressure.
    1. It is independent of the medium in which masses are placed.
  • On what condition, the gravitational force is equal to gravitational constant?

    When the two objects of unit masses are separated by a unit distance, the force of gravitation is equal to the gravitational constant.

  • In which condition the acceleration of a parachute is zero?

    When parachutists jump from a great height, the force of gravity of the earth pulls towards its centre. At the same time, the upthrust of air pushes it upwards. The air resistance increases with the increase in velocity of the parachute. As a result, the force by which parachutes fall down towards the surface of earth will be equal to the upthrust of air, at that time the acceleration becomes zero and it falls at a constant velocity.

This causes the acceleration as zero as a result of which the velocity of the parachute becomes low and balanced. Thus, parachutists are not hurt even when they jump out of a plane.
