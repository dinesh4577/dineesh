Classification of Plants and Animals

  1. Define classification.

    Classification is the process of arranging organisms into groups on the basis of similarities and dissimilarities.

  1. Define taxonomy? Who purposed this term?

    Taxonomy is the branch of biology that deals with the identification, nomenclature, and classification of organisms.

    Carolus Linnaeus is known as the father of taxonomy.

  1. Define the classification of living beings.

    The process of systematic and scientific grouping of living organisms into various groups and subgroups on the basis of their similar or dissimilar characteristics is called the classification of living beings.

  1. Taxonomic Hierarchy

    The arrangement of organisms into different taxonomic groups of different levels in a sequence according to the characteristics is called taxonomic hierarchy.

  1. Importance of Classification
    1. It makes the study of all the organisms easy and systematic.
    1. It helps to know the inter-relationships among different groups of organisms.
    1. It helps to know how the different organisms have evolved with time.
    1. It makes a base for the study of other biological sciences like biodiversity.
  1. Define genus

    Genus is a group of closely related two or more species. The genus 'Felis' consists of small to medium-sized cats and 'canis' consists of dogs, jackals and wolf.

  1. Define species

    Species is a group of living beings which can interbreed and reproduce among themselves and keep their population alive.

  1. Nomenclature

    The system of assigning scientific names to plants and animals is called nomenclature.

    Scientific names are assigned by two words. THe first word refers to genius (generic name) and the second word is species( specific name)
