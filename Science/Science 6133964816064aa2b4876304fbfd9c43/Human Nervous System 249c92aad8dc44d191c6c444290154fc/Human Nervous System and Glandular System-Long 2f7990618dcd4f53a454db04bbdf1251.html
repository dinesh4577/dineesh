  • What is cerebellum or small brain? Describe its structure and functions

    Cerebellum is the second largest part of the brain which occupies 10% of the total weight of brain. It consists of two deeply convoluted hemisphere. It is situated below the posterior portion of the cerebrum and behind the pons varoli.

    Functions

    1. Cerebellum maintains equillibrium and control posture
    1. It makes the body movements smooth, steady and co-ordinated.
    1. It regulates and coordinates the contraction of voluntary muscles and maintains muscular tone.

  • How does nervous system and glandular system together perform the function of control and coordination in human beings?

    Nervous system collects information both from inside and outside of the body with the help of 5 sense organs. It transmits information to organs (effectors), muscles and glands so that they can respond correctly. It also processes information to determine the best response.

    Similary, endocrine system controls growth and development of body, reproduction and functions of various organs of the body.

  • Where are testes located in human body? Name the hormone secreted by testes. Also state the functions of that hormone.

    Testes are oval organs which lie in small muscular pouch (i.e scrotum) outside the abdominal cavity of a man.

    The interstitial cells present in testes produce a male hormone called “ Testosterone”. Testosterone controls the development of secondary sexual characteristics in males like enlargement of penis and scrotum, growth of facial and pubic hair, deeper voice, enlargement of larynx.
